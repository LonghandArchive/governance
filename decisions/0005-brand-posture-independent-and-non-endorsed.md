# 0005 — Brand posture: independent and non-endorsed

Status: Accepted
Date: 2026-04-20

## Context

The Longhand Archive analyses UK public sector data, some of which is produced by organisations the author has professional relationships with. The project is a personal undertaking, not connected to the author's employer, and not endorsed by any UK government department. The Open Government Licence under which the source data is published explicitly forbids implying endorsement.

A project of this shape can drift, through visual choices, linguistic register, or inattentive framing, into looking more institutional than it is. The cost of that drift is twofold: it risks breaching the non-endorsement condition of the OGL, and it creates ambiguity about the project's authority and motivations that is harder to correct than to avoid.

## Decision

The project positions itself as an independent, personal undertaking that analyses public data. Three rules follow from this position.

**The project does not imply official status.** No government-adjacent visual identity. No gov.uk colour palette. No Crown-adjacent iconography. No language that could be read as official guidance, official commentary, or official analysis.

**The project is publicly attributed to its author.** Every public surface — website, README, social presence — identifies the author by name and states that the project is a personal undertaking. The vantage point of the author is not concealed; it is named, so that the reader can make their own assessment.

**The project's tone is observational, not adversarial.** The analytical posture is that of a record-keeper, not a critic. Findings are presented with the evidence that supports them. Language does not editorialise beyond what the data warrants.

## Consequences

Design decisions for any public surface are tested against the first rule. Framing copy on any public surface is tested against the second and third.

When a new surface is created — a new subsite, a new social account, a new publication — the brand posture is re-confirmed rather than inherited silently. The rules are explicit enough to apply without judgement, and the risk of drift is lower for it.
